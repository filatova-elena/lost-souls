You're an orphan. You never knew your parents or where you truly came from. All you have is an old diary—worn, fragile, precious—that somehow ended up with you. The diary was written by someone named Eleanor. She writes about being adopted, about not knowing her real parents. She writes about searching for identity, for belonging. You don't know if Eleanor was your grandmother, great-grandmother, or some other relative. But her words speak to you across the generations. The diary contains a bread recipe—"my grandmother bread recipe," Eleanor calls it. You've been baking this bread your whole life. It is why you chose to be a baker, to feel closer to your family even if you never knew who they were. Recently, you've started having strange feelings. Dreams. The face of a beautiful woman with a pearl necklace calling to you. A sense that you're connected to something bigger than yourself. When you heard about the investigation at the old Montrose mansion, something drew you here. You don't know why, but you feel like you need to be here.
